84- Al-Inshiqâq

1 When the sky is ruptured 2 and listens to its Lord as it rightly must, 3 and when the earth is leveled out 4 and casts out what is in it and becomes empty 5 and listens to its Lord as it rightly must— 6 O man, you are laboring towards your Lord laboriously, then you are bound to meet Him. 7 So as for the one who was brought his recordb in his right hand, 8 he will have an easy reckoning 9 and will return to his family joyful. 10 But as for the one who was brought his record behind his back, 11 he will call for demise 12 and will roast[1875] at the Blaze. 13 Indeed, he used to be joyful among his family; 14 indeed, he thought he would never return. 15 Yes, surely his Lord has always been All-Seeing of him. 16 So, No! I swear by the twilight. 17 By the night and what it covers. 18 By the moon as it grows full— 19 youpl will[1876] surely mount stage by stage. 20 So what is the matter with them that they do not believe? 21 And when the Recital[1877] is recited to them, they do not prostrate themselves? 22 Rather, the deniers disbelieve, 23 but Allah knows better what they hide inside. 24 So give them glad tidings[1878] of a painful punishment— 25 except for those who have attained faith and have done righteous deeds; for them is an unfailing reward.
